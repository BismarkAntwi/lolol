Garlic health benefits

Image by jcomp on Freepik

Garlic, a popular ingredient in many cuisines, is not just known for its unique flavor and aroma but also for its numerous health benefits. For centuries, it has been used as a natural remedy for various ailments. Garlic contains a compound called allicin, which is responsible for its distinctive taste and smell. Allicin is also responsible for most of the health benefits associated with garlic. However, it gets destroyed when it comes into contact with heat. In this post, we'll explore some of the health benefits of garlic.

Boosts Immune System

One of the most well-known health benefits of garlic is its ability to boost the immune system. Garlic contains antioxidants that help to fight off infections, and its antiviral, antibacterial, and antifungal properties make it an excellent ally against colds, flu, and other respiratory infections. According to a study published in the journal Advances in Therapy, consuming garlic supplements can help to reduce the number of colds and the severity of symptoms in adults.

Lowers Blood Pressure

Garlic has been shown to lower blood pressure in people with hypertension. Studies have found that taking garlic supplements or eating garlic regularly can help to reduce both systolic and diastolic blood pressure.

Reduces Cholesterol Levels

Garlic has been shown to lower LDL (bad) cholesterol levels and increase HDL (good) cholesterol levels. This can help to reduce the risk of heart disease.

May Help Prevent Cancer

Garlic is also believed to help reduce the risk of certain types of cancer. According to a review published in the journal Cancer Prevention Research, there is strong evidence to suggest that consuming garlic may help to reduce the risk of several types of cancer, including breast, prostate, and lung cancer .

Improves Bone Health

Garlic contains compounds that may help to improve bone health. Studies have found that garlic supplements can help to increase bone density in women.

May Improve Brain Function

Garlic contains antioxidants that may help to protect the brain from damage caused by free radicals. Studies have also found that garlic may help to improve cognitive function in older adults.

May Improve Athletic Performance

Garlic has been shown to improve exercise performance in people with heart disease. It may also help to improve endurance in athletes.

May Improve Skin Health

Garlic has been found to have anti-inflammatory properties that can help to improve skin health. It may also help to treat skin infections and reduce the severity of acne.

May Reduce the Risk of Alzheimer's Disease and Dementia

Some studies have suggested that garlic may help to reduce the risk of Alzheimer's disease and dementia. The antioxidants in garlic may help to protect the brain from damage caused by free radicals, which are associated with these conditions.

May Help to Treat Hair Loss

Garlic contains compounds that may help to improve hair health and reduce hair loss. Some people apply garlic juice or oil to their scalp to help promote hair growth.

May Help to Improve Digestion

Garlic has been used traditionally as a digestive aid. It may help to stimulate digestion, reduce inflammation in the gut, and improve the balance of gut bacteria.

May Have Anti-inflammatory Properties

May have anti-inflammatory properties: Garlic contains compounds that have been found to have anti-inflammatory properties. This may make it useful for reducing inflammation in the body, which is associated with many chronic health conditions.

May help to reduce the risk of certain cancers: Garlic has been found to have anti-cancer properties and may help to reduce the risk of certain types of cancer, such as breast, prostate, and lung cancer.

May improve insulin sensitivity

Additional, garlic may also help to improve insulin sensitivity and reduce the risk of type 2 diabetes. According to a study published in the Journal of Medicinal Food, consuming garlic supplements was found to improve insulin sensitivity and reduce fasting blood glucose levels in people with type 2 diabetes.

May help to relieve cold and flu symptoms

Garlic has been traditionally used to help relieve symptoms of colds and flu. It may help to reduce the severity and duration of these illnesses.

May improve heart health

has been shown to have a positive effect on heart health by reducing cholesterol levels, improving blood pressure, and reducing the risk of heart disease.

May have antimicrobial properties

Garlic has antimicrobial properties and may help to fight off certain infections, including bacterial, fungal, and viral infections.

Overall, garlic is a nutritious and flavorful ingredient that offers a wide range of health benefits. However, it's important to consume garlic in moderation and talk to your healthcare provider before adding it to your diet, especially if you're taking any medications or have underlying health conditions.

Side Effects of Garlic

  • Bad breath: Garlic can cause bad breath that may last for several hours after consumption.
  • Digestive issues: Eating large amounts of garlic can cause digestive issues, such as bloating, gas, and diarrhea.
  • Skin irritation: Applying raw garlic directly to the skin can cause irritation, redness, and even blistering in some people.
  • Bleeding: Garlic can thin the blood, which can increase the risk of bleeding in some individuals.
  • Interactions with medications: Garlic can interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and some antibiotics, which can affect their effectiveness.

If you experience any of these side effects or have concerns about consuming garlic, it's important to talk to your healthcare provider.


Garlic Statistics

According to a meta-analysis of 39 studies, garlic can significantly reduce blood pressure in people with hypertension. The analysis found that taking garlic supplements for 12 weeks reduced systolic blood pressure by an average of 7.7 mmHg and diastolic blood pressure by an average of 5.0 mmHg. (Source: Nutrition Journal)

A study of 42,000 women found that those who ate garlic and other Allium vegetables (such as onions and leeks) had a significantly lower risk of colon cancer compared to those who ate these vegetables less frequently. The study found that women who ate these vegetables at least once a week had a 35% lower risk of colon cancer compared to those who ate them less than once a month. (Source: American Journal of Clinical Nutrition)

A study of 80 elderly patients with high cholesterol found that taking garlic supplements for 12 weeks reduced total cholesterol levels by an average of 15.6%, LDL cholesterol levels by an average of 17.9%, and triglyceride levels by an average of 10.3%. (Source: Journal of Nutrition)

A study of 120 patients with osteoporosis found that taking garlic supplements for 6 months improved bone density and reduced the risk of fractures. The study found that the group taking garlic supplements had a significant increase in bone mineral density compared to the placebo group. (Source: Phytotherapy Research)

A study of 60 patients with type 2 diabetes found that taking garlic supplements for 12 weeks improved insulin sensitivity and reduced fasting blood sugar levels. The study found that the group taking garlic supplements had a significant reduction in fasting blood sugar levels compared to the placebo group. (Source: Journal of Medicinal Food)

These statistics highlight the significant health benefits of garlic and the potential impact it can have on various health conditions. However, it's important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the effects of garlic on health.
